Rules I keep for myself, short enough to remember and stubborn enough to survive most weeks.

When in doubt, do the more boring thing. It is almost always correct and it is always cheaper.

If you find yourself writing a helper you only call once, inline it and delete the helper. Abstractions are a tax; pay only when the savings are obvious.

Sleep first, ship later. A tired engineer is an expensive engineer.

Do not argue with someone who gets paid to be wrong.

The last 10% of a feature is the 90% that users will see. Budget accordingly.

If the test is hard to write, the design is hard to use. Do not blame the test.

When you catch yourself reaching for an LLM to get out of thinking, think for one more minute first. You will be surprised how often that minute is enough.
